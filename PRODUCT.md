# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indonesian learners studying Japanese — from beginners memorizing hiragana/katakana to intermediate learners preparing for JLPT N2–N5. Used at home on desktop or mobile during self-study sessions. The user is in a task: memorizing, drilling, reviewing. Distraction is the enemy.

## Product Purpose

Japanese Master is a self-study tool that covers the full progression from kana to kanji to JLPT vocabulary. It replaces scattered apps by putting hiragana quizzes, kanji flashcards, JLPT prep, and vocabulary drilling in one place. Success looks like a learner moving confidently through levels without hitting UI friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, encouraging. Like a patient tutor who knows their subject cold. Not a gamified achievement machine, not a clinical reference tool — somewhere between Notion and a well-designed textbook. Never talks down. Never shouts.

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS: blue-gradient hero sections, hero metrics, enterprise-speak
- Generic AI-generated: cream/sand body background, tiled icon+text cards, tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section, numbered scaffolding (01/02/03) used decoratively
- Duolingo-style: owls, streaks, excessive celebration, patronizing encouragement

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** When studying, the UI should be invisible. No chrome, no decoration fighting the kanji for attention.
2. **Confidence through consistency.** Same button shapes, same form controls, same spacing rhythm across every screen. Familiar patterns build trust.
3. **Warmth in the right places.** Encouragement shows up in copy and states, not in decoration. An empty state teaches, a success state celebrates briefly, then gets out of the way.
4. **Every element earns its place.** If removing something doesn't hurt, remove it. The kanji is the hero.
5. **Mobile-first, not mobile-degraded.** Flashcard sessions happen on phones. Layouts must be designed for thumb reach and one-handed use, not retrofitted from desktop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text)
- Reduced motion support: animations must have prefers-reduced-motion fallbacks
- Japanese text requires Noto Sans JP at appropriate sizes — never drop below 14px for kanji
